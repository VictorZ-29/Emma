Feelings & reactions

The words for how you actually are — knackered, thrilled, fed up, can’t-be-bothered. Adjectives are given in the feminine, since that’s the form you’ll say; the notes give the masculine.

How to use the deck

Tap a card to flip it; tap the speaker to hear it. Use ‹ › to move, Shuffle to mix, and Got it for the ones you own. Two minutes a day beats an hour on Sunday.

Put them to work

Chuis crevée, mais trop contente de te voir.

“I’m shattered, but so happy to see you.” Two cards, one very real sentence.

J’ai la flemme, on reste à la maison ?

“I can’t be bothered — shall we stay in?” A national mood, now available to you.

The avoir family

Notice how many feelings are things you have: avoir la flemme, avoir hâte, avoir peur, en avoir marre, avoir le moral. If a card starts with avoir, drill it as a whole chunk with j’ai…
