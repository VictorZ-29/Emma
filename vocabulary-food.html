Put them to work

Survival phrases for a café

Une table pour deux, s’il vous plaît.

“A table for two, please.”

Je voudrais un café, s’il vous plaît.

“I’d like a coffee, please.” Je voudrais… is your all-purpose polite “I’d like…”.

L’addition, s’il vous plaît.

“The bill, please.” The single most useful sentence in any restaurant.

☕

Café tip

In France, un café means a small espresso. If you want something longer and milky, ask for un crème (or un café au lait at breakfast). A “coffee with hot water on the side” is un café allongé.
