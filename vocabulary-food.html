Food & going out

Eating out in France is a social event, and these are its words — from l’apéro to l’addition. Every card shows the noun with its article, so the gender comes along for free.

How to use the deck

Tap a card to flip it; tap the speaker to hear it. Use ‹ › to move, Shuffle to mix, and Got it for the ones you own. This deck pairs with lesson 9, Out in the world.

Put them to work

On se met en terrasse et on prend l’apéro ?

“Shall we sit outside and have a drink?” Possibly the most French sentence ever assembled.

Je vais prendre le plat du jour, et une carafe d’eau.

“I’ll have the dish of the day, and a jug of (free) tap water.” Ordering like a regular.

C’était délicieux. L’addition, s’il vous plaît !

“That was delicious. The bill, please!” Compliment first — it always lands well.

Café decoder

Un café = a small espresso. Milky and generous = un crème; stretched with water = un allongé; with a dash of milk = une noisette. Order « un crème » and you’ll sound like you’ve lived there for years.
