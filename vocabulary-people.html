Family & relationships

The people words — friends, partners, family, neighbours — including the casual ones (pote, mec) that textbooks skip and conversations are full of.

How to use the deck

Tap a card to flip it; tap the speaker to hear it. Read the notes on this deck especially — the friend/partner distinctions (un copain vs mon copain) genuinely matter.

Put them to work

Je te présente Léa, une pote du boulot.

“This is Léa, a mate from work.” Je te présente… is the casual introduction formula.

On est très proches, ma sœur et moi.

“My sister and I are very close.” The …, X et moi tag is a very French way to add the detail after.

The copain/copine trap

With mon/ma it usually means partner: « mon copain » = my boyfriend. With un/une it’s just a friend: « un copain ». Introducing a male friend? « un pote » is unambiguous and safe.
